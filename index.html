Android: Kiosk Mode (Without Root)

Since Android doesn't let you have full control over the whole operating system, you are quite limited as to what you can do to fully "lock-down" your device with your application (i.e. Kiosk mode). However, there are some workarounds to this if you spend enough time to tinker around. To simulate kiosk mode on Android for a full lockdown on an Android application, there are a few things you need to control. Learn more...

  1. Status bar
  2. Back button
  3. Home button
  4. Multitask button
  5. Power and volume button

The easiest way to hide the status bar dropdown is to either hide it completely by setting the application to full screen

However, if you do not want the user to fully deny users access to the status bar (to navigate out of the application) there is a simply hacky way of achieving this by creating an overlay that would rest on top of your application that will consume all touch inputs from the user (i.e. swiping down the status bar).



Android: WiFi Connection

Android allows you to connect to various scanned WiFi connections available in your area through settings. However, if you need WiFi scanning and connection integrated within your app, an easy way to do it is to use Android's built in WifiManager in API's higher than 28 or Android Pie. Android: Bluetooth Connection

Android allows you total control over available bluetooth connections in settings, but you can also create your own in app bluetooth connection manager where you can scan for all bluetooth device (or a specific bluetooth device), choose a device to pair to, and remove bonded device(s).
